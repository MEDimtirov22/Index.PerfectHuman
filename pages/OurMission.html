Mission

Starlight, an advanced spacecraft, is on a groundbreaking mission to the interstellar star Proxima Centauri. Equipped with cutting-edge technology, it aims to deploy the three rovers on Proxima Centauri B to collect samples and conduct extensive research, pushing the boundaries of human exploration

Mini Perseverance

A compact rover, inspired by NASA's larger version used on February 18, 2014. It retains key functionalities while reducing size and weight. It explores Proxima Centauri b, collects data, and advances our knowledge. Its smaller form enables easy transportation and maneuverability in challenging terrains.

Spacecraft Tech

Solar Power

Introducing a set of four solar panels, each measuring 12x13 meters, designed to harness sunlight and charge the spacecraft's battery. These solar panels efficiently convert sunlight into electrical energy. With a combined output of several megawatts, they provide a sustainable power source.

Battery

We are using the revolutionary battery designed to power the engine of the spacecraft. With its capacity of 3,000 kilowatt-hours and advanced technology, this battery ensures a reliable and sustained energy source. Its long-lasting capabilities allow the engine to operate for extended periods.

Electric Thruster

The spacecraft powered by an advanced Spacecraft Electric Propulsion engine. With the ability to accelerate using electric fields, Starlight can an incredible speed of 90 km/s. This technology offers greater efficiency, longer missions and revolutionizes space exploration.

Droid Carrier

Starlight is a groundbreaking transportation system that revolutionizes exploration by simultaneously transporting up to four mini rovers. With its enhanced flexibility and mobility, it expands our scientific capabilities, pushing the boundaries of knowledge and unlocking new frontiers.
